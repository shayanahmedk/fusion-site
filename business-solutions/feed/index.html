Since the onset of COVID-19 back in early 2020, public health authorities have made efforts to detect, monitor and slow the spread of the disease.

A key part of this process is contact tracing. According to the U.S. Centers for Disease Control & Prevention (CDC), contact tracing has helped slow the spread of the virus.

What is contact tracing, and how is contact tracing done? Most people are vaguely aware of contact tracing, and TeleDirect’s call center professionals, which help spearhead one of the premier healthcare call center platforms available anywhere, put together this quick reference to answer any questions you have.

 

What is Contact Tracing?

How does contact tracing work? Simply put, contact tracing is the process of analyzing, identifying and tracking any people who have come in contact with an infected person.

This process involves both inbound & outbound communication, with collaboration between health authorities and the general public. As you might expect, medical establishments with fully capable, reliable contact centers have been able to conduct contact tracing activities much more efficiently than those without one.

TeleDirect’s hotline call center is also a useful tool to handle critical, time-sensitive communication between patients and healthcare providers.

 

How Is Contact Tracing Done?

How does contact tracing actually work? Here’s a general overview of the procedure:

  • The index case of COVID-19 is identified when the patient notifies health authorities.
  • This person is asked questions about their recent whereabouts, recent contacts, etc.
  • People who have come into contact with the index case are contacted and asked further questions.
  • The index case and any close contacts are given counsel, treatment advice, screening, etc.
  • If required, the index case and any close associates are advised to quarantine or limit their public appearances.
  • All data is logged in public health databases for a comprehensive picture of the disease’s spread (cities, towns, townships, states, etc.)

Tools like surveys’ & feedback are critical in piecing together the overall health puzzle associated with any outbreak.

The Basics: Telehealth Meaning, Benefits of Telehealth & More

The simplest definition of telehealth is essentially the presentation and distribution of medical information from anywhere in the world. Medical checkups, prescriptions, review of personal patient info like x-rays, general healthcare updates related to patient care…you get the idea.

What do patients need to know about how to use telehealth? The tools are simple – a secure internet connection and working webcam at the bare minimum. Some healthcare organizations will use popular videoconferencing apps like Zoom, or proprietary applications to further enhance protections and data security to meet HIPAA requirements.

Since the technology is new, there are plenty of telehealth benefits and challenges to consider. On the positive side, the pure convenience of telehealth is hard to beat. Plus, telehealth helps limit the costs associated with in person visits. One of the obvious benefits of telehealth is the remote configuration, which helps keep vulnerable patients protected from infectious diseases away from home.

However, there are a few hurdles the healthcare community is still working through with telehealth. For one, how do telehealth services align with traditional reservations? Given staffing shortages, outbound communication is more important than ever to keep patients engaged and properly informed. And since many patients prefer in person visits, how does telehealth impact patient retention for healthcare enterprises?
